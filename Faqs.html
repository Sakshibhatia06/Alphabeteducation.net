Frequently Asked Questions

We bring to you answers to several questions that are asked frequently by our applicants planning to study abroad. If you have a question that is not answered here, please send it to us through WhatsApp or email and we will not only respond to you promptly but also include it here in our future updates.

There are a number of ranking platforms abroad that rate universities based on multiple factors. They include QS, US News, Bloomberg, Times Higher Education and others. We monitor these platforms regularly and share the outcomes with our applicants.

Yes. If you are unsure about which career line to pursue, our resident career counsellor will work with you and determine your academic and career track. The application cannot start until such a time that you are absolutely sure about your career goals.

Yes. We have empanelled several highly qualified and experienced tutors who will prepare you for various standardized tests. This service is delivered one-on-one online by our highly experienced trainers through Zoom. Tests with which we can help you include AP, GMAT, GRE, TOEFL, IELTS, SAT, ACT, MCAT, LSAT and others. The tutor will first assess your capabilities and potential and subsequently carve a customized training plan for you. This service is available to enrolled applicants only and not as a stand-alone service.

Yes. since universities around the world are mostly not inexpensive, any scholarship support you will receive will help greatly. We focus on scholarships offered by universities to which you have been admitted. There is a different process for third party scholarships granted by trusts, NGOs and foundations. Please ask us during an interaction and we will explain this process to you in comprehensive detail.

Yes. Once you set up your account on a university's website and share your login credentials with us, we will take the ball and run with it. You are always welcome to log into your application portal and review application pages. We will also ask you to enter data in those fields for which we might not have the information necessary. Examples include:

From which university did your father graduate?
What is your mother's date of birth?
What is the nature of your father's employment?
Have your parents attended college?
Universities like to go beyond your academic record and learn more about you as an individual. It is important that we start early with this process because good things take time to happen and incubate.

Yes. Student visa support is included in all our packages for one country. Our visa success rate is near 100%.

Our post admission service spectrum optionally transcends your initial admission and covers you during your entire academic journey much like an insurance policy. Service delivery is determined on a case-by-case basis. Some examples include:
Post Arrival accommodation assistance
Help with registering for courses online well before the start of term
Help with choosing electives
Internship assistance
Assistance with your CPT and SSN processes
Assistance with university scholarship applications
Roommate recommendations
Regular resume updates
Post-arrival visa document processing such as the PGWP in Canada, STEMOPT in the U.S., and Skilled Workers Visa in the UK.
